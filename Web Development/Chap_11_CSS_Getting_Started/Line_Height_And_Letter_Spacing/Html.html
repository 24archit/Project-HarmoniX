Seven Wonders of the World

The Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza, located in Egypt, is the only surviving ancient wonder. It is a marvel of engineering and construction and is the largest of the three pyramids on the Giza Plateau.

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon, often considered a legend, were said to be a remarkable feat of ancient engineering with lush greenery suspended in the air. Their existence remains a topic of debate among historians.

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia, created by the Greek sculptor Phidias, was a giant seated figure of the god Zeus. It stood in the temple at Olympia and was renowned for its artistic and cultural significance.

The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus

The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus, located in present-day Turkey, was a grand temple dedicated to the goddess Artemis. It was known for its impressive size and stunning architecture.

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus was a monumental tomb built for Mausolus, the Persian satrap of Caria, and his wife Artemisia. The term "mausoleum" originated from this impressive structure.

The Colossus of Rhodes

The Colossus of Rhodes was a gigantic bronze statue of the sun god Helios that stood at the entrance of the harbor of Rhodes. It was one of the tallest statues of the ancient world, but it no longer exists today.

The Lighthouse of Alexandria

The Lighthouse of Alexandria was a marvel of ancient engineering, standing on the island of Pharos in Alexandria, Egypt. It served as a guiding light for sailors and was one of the tallest structures of the time.
